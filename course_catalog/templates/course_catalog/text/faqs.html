Frequently Asked Questions

The questions you've asked, and those that you didn't.

  • Will Qcumber replace SOLUS one day?

    Is that a trick question?
    SOLUS serves a lot of purposes that Qcumber makes no effort to address.
    (P.S. You should always confirm course information with SOLUS. Qcumber is the unofficial course catalogue. SOLUS is the official one.)

  • What's your favourite feature of SOLUS?

    The way you can't open tabs. Scratch that. It's how some sections only show up in search results, and some sections only show up in the course catalog. Mmm.

  • I have a question about a course/subject/concentration.

    Qcumber doesn't attempt to offer guidance on what courses you should take. You should contact the department for that kind of information.

  • I have a link to a course webpage, syllabus, or registration tip for you to post.

    Always appreciated! We're working on including those on the site, so you can send them here, or save them until we release that feature.

  • How do you feel about sea cucumbers?

    Posers.

  • What new features can we look forward to?

    • personalized shortlists for subjects/courses of interest
    • a tool to help you find that elusive final course
    • access to textbook information
    • course ratings
    • easy-to-view, printable timetable

  • How does Qcumber work?

    Fairies.

  • No really.

    Ok fine. Information is obtained via an awesome tool called Selenium Remote Control, and then put in a database. I then serve it to you using Django for your easy-browsing pleasure! Aren't you glad you asked?

  • Can you let me know when new features come out?

    Sure! Like us on facebook, or follow us on twitter.

  • Are you making money off this?

    No. Actually, I'm paying for hosting and domain registration costs myself, because I'm not a fan of ads. Let me know if you want to buy me lunch! :)

  • How can I help?

    Feedback, suggestions, and favourite recipes welcome HERE. If you're a developer, email HERE.

  • What's your favourite way to eat cucumber?

    On the cob. Maybe with salt and pepper.

  • Does Qcumber mean anything?

    Quasi-Cylindrical Underappreciated Mostly-Beloved Edible Refreshment.
